Fried chicken

History:

Fried Chicken was developed when several differnt cooking methods from Africans and Scottish and mixing them with American ingredients. Enslaved people in early America used these techiniques ceating the first forms of Fried Chicken known for its crunch and flavor. Throughout the years, fried chicken began rising in popularity especially after WW2. Other cultures began making their own version of fried chicken like the Koreans making the skin super crispy and adding flavorful sauces. But the most popular and what we'll be cooking today is the classic Southern Fried Chicken.

Ingredients:

Chicken:
8 chicken pieces (legs, thighs, wings, or breasts)
Marinade:
2 cups buttermilk
1 tbsp hot sauce (optional)
1 tsp salt
1 tsp black pepper
1 tsp paprika
1 tsp garlic powder
Coating:
2 cups all-purpose flour
½ cup cornstarch (optional for extra crunch)
1 tbsp salt
2 tsp black pepper
2 tsp paprika
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp onion powder
½–1 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)

Steps:

Step 1: Mix the marinade and coating. Then add the chicken to the marinade. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes and at most 3 hours.
Step 2: Add the marinated chicken to the coating and dip it again in the marinade and back to the coating. This is to ensure that we have crispy chicken skin. Step 3: Once all the chickens are coated, Fry each piece of chicken for 8 minutes on high heat.
